How to use the desk

This is a first-pass filter for player-bookings markets. It does not pick players. It tells you which fixtures and which teams carry the most card risk, so your per-fixture research starts in the right place.

Tiers, off cards against

  • Target, 2.0 cards against per game and above.
  • Mid, 1.5 to 1.9.
  • Fade, 1.4 and below.
  • Combustible (flame), 2.0 plus cards against and 12.5 plus fouls made. High cards and high fouls together.

Matchup heat

The heat score is transparent on purpose. For a fixture it is:

heat = (avgCards / 3.1 × 6) + (avgFouls / 15.4 × 4), clamped 0 to 10

avgCards is the two-team average of cards against. avgFouls is the two-team average of fouls made. Cards are weighted 60 per cent, fouls 40 per cent. The divisors 3.1 and 15.4 are the highest cards-against and fouls-made in the field, so the score is normalised against the most extreme side in the tournament.

Jeopardy adjustment

Heat measures how two teams play. Jeopardy measures what is at stake. The desk reads the live group table and gives each side a status: Through if they have already qualified, Win to qualify or Third-place scrap if they are still fighting, Slim chance or Outside shot if the maths is against them, Eliminated if they are out. Top two qualify plus the eight best third-placed teams, so a side that can still reach about four points stays alive.

Two desperate sides push the heat up by as much as 1.5, two safe or eliminated sides pull it down by up to 1.0, and one motivated side against a settled one is roughly neutral. The adjustment is halved before the final round, when the table has not yet hardened, and shown in full on the last group games. It moves on its own as results come in.

Goalscorer markets

The Goals tab is the scoring counterpart to Matchups. Pick a fixture and it shows a goal climate, the two teams averaged over 2.5 and both-teams-score rates, plus an expected-goals readout:

exp goals = (A goals for + B goals against) / 2 + (B goals for + A goals against) / 2

Below that, each team's goal threats are ranked by a threat score, goals per 90 and expected goals per 90 weighted 60 to 40, with shots on target per 90 shown alongside. Same club form and international toggle as the bookings layer. It points you at the likely scorers, it does not price the market.

The Scorers tab is a Golden Boot board across the whole field, with three views, top threats for the tournament, top scorer for each team, and top scorer for each group. It ranks by the same threat score with low-sample players removed, and shows season goals for context. It is a rate proxy, it does not model how far a team progresses or penalties.

Where the numbers come from

Team rates are per-game figures from ScoutingStats country form, one card per team, covering all internationals. The per-fixture player layer has a club form and international toggle, both from ScoutingStats. Club form, the 2025-26 club season with broad league coverage, ranks every squad player by a risk score, yellow rate per 90 weighted double plus fouls per 90. International ranks the same way on national-team form for 2026, with hosts Mexico, USA and Canada excluded since they played no qualifiers. Each team also shows its qualifying-campaign card leaders. None of the player data feeds the tiers or heat. Referee figures are blended estimates with varying sample sizes. A confirmed-starter and referee blend layers on once line-ups and appointments are known.

Known limits

  • All 48 teams now carry form data. Sample sizes vary by team, from 12 games to 30. Smaller samples are noisier.
  • Cesar Ramos cannot referee Mexico on nationality. The watchlist notes this.
  • Referee per-game rates from short tournament samples will read high. Treat them as direction, not precision.
